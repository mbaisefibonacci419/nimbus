A concept-validation playground for
TurboTax AI-powered tax prep.

The problem

We have big ambitions for AI in TurboTax — the Digital Assistant, the co-pilot, agentic filing. But validating new UX concepts at the speed of the AI landscape is hard when the only way to test an idea is to build it inside the production codebase. By the time a concept clears scoping, design, and eng allocation, the window to learn from it may have passed.

"What if a PM could validate a product concept end-to-end —
before writing a single PRD?"

Ugo Ibecheozor  ·  May 2026

What I built

A working prototype — not a mockup — that covers ~85–90% of individual DIY filers. Real tax engine, real AI agent mode, real document import. 90+ tax features across all 50 states, validated by 6,100+ tests. Built from public IRS sources — no proprietary Intuit data or code.

17 feature concepts, each mapped to S2C, ARPC, PRS, or C2C — with IXP experiment sketches ready to hand off.

Why it matters internally

Nimbus lets PMs validate product hypotheses against a realistic filing experience before committing eng resources. Every concept in this deck was explored, iterated, and stress-tested in the prototype — not theorized in a doc. The ideas that survive are sharper, the experiment designs are grounded, and the conversations with eng start at "here's how it works" instead of "imagine if."

Search That Gets
You There

The gap today

The search bar routes everything through chat. But "Go to W-2" isn't a question — it's a navigational intent. "HSA limit" is informational. "Add a dependent" is an action. Routing all of these through chat adds friction for the majority of queries.

Customer insight

When search becomes a conversation, it slows down the very customers who know what they want. The right tool for the right intent means faster time-to-answer for the majority, and deeper AI help for the questions that need it.

See Your Taxes,
Don't Just File Them

Interactive visual breakdowns available at any point during filing: where your money goes, how brackets work, a waterfall from income to refund, and line-by-line traces back to the IRS source.

Combats the most common misconception about marginal tax rates. Customers see their effective rate and understand why moving into a "higher bracket" doesn't mean all their income is taxed at that rate.

Customer insight

Tax anxiety is both a PRS problem and a completion problem. Customers who feel confused by what's happening to their money are less likely to complete and less likely to recommend. "Your effective rate is 14.2% — here's why" is more reassuring than a number on a screen.

PRS S2C Reduces tax anxiety; confident customers complete and recommend
What customers see
📊 Tax flow diagram — where your money goes
📈 Bracket chart — marginal vs effective rate
📉 Waterfall — income to refund breakdown
🔍 Trace tree — every line linked to its IRS source
Addressable market

All DIY filers — new customers (highest tax anxiety) and complex filers benefit most. PRS improvement compounds into C2C over time.

The AI Co-Pilot:
Agent Mode

Instead of answering one question at a time, Agent Mode walks the customer through their entire return via conversation. 17 specialized skills cover every section — personal info, each income type, deductions, credits, state taxes, review, and finish.

Guiding principle

Speed through known data. Explore through unknown value.

When the customer has a W-2, the agent confirms it in one exchange. When the customer might be eligible for a credit they didn't know about, the agent explains the benefit and walks them through it.

Where this fits

TurboTax already has the Digital Assistant and the explanations agent. That work was a stepping stone — answering questions about one screen at a time.

Agent Mode is where that path leads: from answering questions about a single screen to guiding the entire return.

The customer can switch between Agent, Interview, and Forms at any time. Progress carries across all three modes.

Addressable market

Experienced DIY filers, gig workers, and document-forward customers who want to get through their return fast — a growing segment as AI expectations rise.

A Winback Play:
Competitive Import

Upload a prior-year return from H&R Block, TaxAct, FreeTaxUSA, or any other provider as a PDF. The system detects which product generated it, extracts all data, and pre-fills the current year.

The barrier today

Switching cost is a retention moat for competitors. If a customer used H&R Block last year and has to re-enter everything manually, many won't bother trying TurboTax.

Customer insight

The switching barrier drops from "re-enter everything" to "upload and review." One PDF, one confirmation screen, done.
